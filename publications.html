Individualization of atrial tachycardia models for clinical applications: Performance of fiber-independent model

Jiyue He, Arkady Pertsov, John Bullinga, Rahul Mangharam

IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Engineering, Jul 2023, doi: 10.1109/TBME.2023.3298003. TBME journal is ranked No. 3 by Google Scholar. This paper received the Featured Article Award.

The study presents a comparison between fiber-independent and fiber-inclusive models for personalizing cardiac arrhythmia simulations, particularly atrial tachycardias. Using DT-MRI data from 7 ex-vivo hearts, it evaluates the accuracy of left atrium activation maps across 51 focal and rotor arrhythmia cases. Findings reveal the isotropic model, adjusted through tuned diffusion coefficients, achieves high accuracy (96% for focal, 93% for rotor arrhythmias), suggesting its potential for clinical applications in cardiac ablation procedures without requiring direct myocardial fiber measurements.

Fiber organization has little effect on electrical activation patterns during focal arrhythmias in the left atrium

Jiyue He, Arkady Pertsov, Elizabeth Cherry, Flavio Fenton, Caroline Roney, Steven Niederer, Zirui Zang, Rahul Mangharam

IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Engineering, vol. 70, no. 5, pp. 1611-1621, May 2023, doi: 10.1109/TBME.2022.3223063. TBME journal is ranked No. 3 by Google Scholar. This paper received the Featured Article Award.

The study presents a chimeric model study on the left atrium, examining the influence of myocardial fiber organization on electrical activation patterns during focal arrhythmias. Findings indicate that despite significant variability in fiber organization, there's a minor effect on spatio-temporal activation patterns during regular pacing. This suggests that intricate modeling of myocardial fiber orientation might not be crucial for accurately predicting electrical activation patterns in clinical settings for focal arrhythmias in the left atrium, potentially simplifying cardiac conduction modeling.

Real-time atrial tachycardia ablation guidance with a left atrium model

Jiyue He, Arkady Pertsov, Rahul Mangharam

Heart Rhythm Society Abstract, volume 20, issue 5, May 2023, doi: 10.1016/j.hrthm.2023.03.343. HRS is a top academic/clinical organization of heart rhythm in the world.

The study introduces an innovative electrophysiological model for real-time guidance in atrial tachycardia ablation, utilizing a fast parameter-tuning approach without the need for fiber data. This model demonstrates high accuracy in reproducing arrhythmia sources, verified against retrospective patient data and ex-vivo in-silico simulations, offering a significant advancement in the precision and efficiency of ablation procedures.

Tachycardia activation pattern predictivity of a fiber-independent left atrium model

Jiyue He, Arkady Pertsov, John Bullinga, Rahul Mangharam

Cardiac Physiome Society 2023. CPS is a global conference on cardiac modeling.

The study introduces a fiber-independent heart model for predicting tachycardia activation patterns in the left atrium without needing fiber data, showing an average low error rate in activation times and arrhythmia source locations. This model, using tuned diffusion coefficients, suggests potential for enhancing cardiac ablation procedures by accurately identifying arrhythmia sources without the lengthy process of fiber data acquisition.

Patient-specific heart model towards atrial fibrillation

Jiyue He, Arkady Pertsov, Sanjay Dixit, Katie Walsh, Eric Toolan, Rahul Mangharam

Proceedings of the ACM/IEEE 12th International Conference on Cyber-Physical Systems, New York, NY, USA, pp. 33-43, May 2021, doi: 10.1145/3450267.3450532. ICCPS is a large-scale international conference.

The study presents a computational model designed to enhance catheter ablation treatment for atrial fibrillation by accurately simulating heart activation patterns. Trained with data from 15 patients, the model assists in pinpointing arrhythmia sources, showcasing an average local activation time error of 5.47 ms for sinus rhythm and 10.97 ms for flutter and tachycardia, indicating its precision in identifying potential treatment targets.

Electroanatomic mapping to determine scar regions in patients with atrial fibrillation

Jiyue He, Kuk Jin Jang, Katie Walsh, Jackson Liang, Sanjay Dixit, Rahul Mangharam

The 41st Annual International Conference of the IEEE Engineering in Medicine and Biology Society, Berlin, Germany, pp. 5941-5944, Jul 2019, doi: 10.1109/EMBC.2019.8856704. EMBS is a large-scale international conference.

The study introduces a novel method for electroanatomic mapping in atrial fibrillation, enhancing scar region identification using omni-directional bipolar voltages and Gaussian process regression. Tested on clinical patients data, it showed improved sensitivity and specificity in low voltage area mapping.
