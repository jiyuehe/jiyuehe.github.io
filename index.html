I am a Computer Scientist specializing in mathematical modeling, machine learning, algorithm development, and data analysis. I am currently a researcher at the University of California, San Francisco (UCSF), and I earned my PhD from the University of Pennsylvania (UPenn).

Programming languages I am proficient in: Python, C++, MATLAB, HTML, JavaScript, and CSS.

My recent focus is on integrating electrical imaging technologies and intracardiac echocardiography with artificial intelligence to further enhance arrhythmia source detections and characterize heart rhythm disorders.

I have developed a Python heart simulator capable of reproducing patient-specific focal and rotor arrhythmias, as well as generating atrial fibrillation (AFib) simulations. The movie above shows an example of an AFib simulation.

I am developing an electro-mechanical heart simulator written in C++ that models both electrical wave propagation and mechanical contractions. The movies above show examples of simulations in the left atrium and the ventricles.

In addition, I analyze clinical data obtained from arrhythmia ablation procedures, which are minimally invasive surgeries. This allows me to apply methods developed in silico to real-world clinical settings to help improve patient care. Below are details of the research projects I have completed.

Atrial Fibrillation Ablation Challenges
A heart with atrial fibrillation experiences irregular electrical activations, leading to inefficient blood pumping. Ablation is the most common treatment.

Patient-specific Digital Heart for Arrhythmia Ablation
Develop heart models to simulate patient-specific electrical activation behaviors, derive the discrete form the heart model equations for programming, show arrhythmia simulation examples, patient-specific parameter tuning, and validation with patient data.

Myocardial Fiber Effects on Arrhythmia Patterns
Myocardial fiber data is not available for clinical arrhythmia ablation. We would like to find a way to compensate for the lack of fiber data.

Clinical Data Processing and Arrhythmia Source Detection
Describe the tools, user interfaces, and algorithms we developed for processing clinical data.
